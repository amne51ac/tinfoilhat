ABOUT RF ATTENUATION

RF attenuation measures how effectively a material reduces the power of radio frequency signals. We express this reduction in decibels (dB), where positive values indicate signal blocking and negative values indicate signal amplification.

MEASUREMENT

We measure RF attenuation by comparing signal strength before and after passing through shielding material. The difference, calculated in decibels, gives us the attenuation value. We test across different frequencies to determine overall shielding effectiveness.

FARADAY SHIELDS

A Faraday shield is a conductive enclosure that blocks external electric fields by redistributing electric charges on its surface. For optimal shielding, the material must be conductive, continuous (no gaps), and ideally grounded to dissipate induced charges.

Any opening in a Faraday shield can significantly reduce its effectiveness:

Openings larger than 1/10th of the wavelength allow significant signal leakage.
Multiple small holes are better than one large hole of the same total area.
Mesh screens can provide ventilation while maintaining shielding if the mesh size is small enough.

PRACTICAL APPLICATIONS

RF shielding has many practical applications:

RF-blocking wallets protect credit cards and passports from wireless skimming.
Faraday cages prevent interference in medical imaging rooms.
RF shielding bags protect electronic devices during forensic analysis.
