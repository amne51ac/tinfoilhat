About the Competition

The Tinfoil Hat Competition is a unique event that tests the signal attenuation properties of contestant-made tinfoil hats. Using professional RF testing equipment, we measure how effectively your hat blocks various radio frequencies.

How It Works

1. Create Your Hat

> Design and build your tinfoil hat using any materials you choose

> Be creative and innovative in your approach to RF shielding!

2. Testing Process

> Your hat will be tested using a HackRF One device

> We measure signal attenuation from 88.5 MHz to 6 GHz

3. Results & Scoring

> We compare signal strength with and without the hat

> Calculate average attenuation in decibels (dB)

About RF Attenuation

Measurement

> We measure RF attenuation by comparing signal strength before and after passing through shielding material

> The difference, calculated in decibels, gives us the attenuation value

> We test across different frequencies to determine overall shielding effectiveness

Faraday Shields

> A Faraday shield is a conductive enclosure that blocks external electric fields

> For optimal shielding, the material must be conductive, continuous (no gaps), and ideally grounded

> Any opening in a Faraday shield can significantly reduce its effectiveness

Key Points

> Openings larger than 1/10th of the wavelength allow significant signal leakage

> Multiple small holes are better than one large hole of the same total area

> Mesh screens can provide ventilation while maintaining shielding if the mesh size is small enough
